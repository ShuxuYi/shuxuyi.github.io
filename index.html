Shu-Xu YI

I am a postdoctoral fellow in the University of Hong Kong. Previously I was a PhD student in the Unveristy of Chinese Academy of Sciences. I'm interested in pulsars, which are a kind of dead stars.

With size of a small city, a pulsar has the mass more than that of the sun. Therefore it's the idea place for studying superstrong gravity. Besides, the magnetic field of a pulsar is billions of Telsa (the world record on Earth is less than 100 Telsa). And the pulsar is fast spinning, as a result the speed at the surface of its magnetosphere is close to the speed of light.

The facinating celestial labortory provides incomparable advantages to study fundamental physics, such as the Theory of Relativities/alternative gravity theories, nuclear and particle physics, plasma physics, etc.

Pulsars are also the most accurate clocks and cosmic light houses. Astronomers can use pulsars as tools to study wide topics in astrophysics. For instance detection of gravitational waves with pulsar timing array, accurate measurement of masses of stars in pulsar binaries, etc.
