Research Interests

My research interests lie on three strong-linked aspects:

1) pulsar timing;

2) pulsar/binary emission mechansims;

3) gravitational waves.

Pulsar timing

The basic idea of pulsar timing is to measure the time of every pulse from the pulsar, and to find out how the time intervals between succesive pulses vary from time to time. Since pulsars are very stable tick-tocking clocks, the variation is tiny.

There are a lot of things which can give rise to these tiny variation of the pulses arrival time. In other words, pulsar timing can be used as a probe to study many aspects in physics and astrophysics (see my Ph.D thesis for a review). For instances, the evolution of the magnetic field of neutron stars (Yi & Zhang, 2015), and the gravitational waves passing between the Earth and pulsars (Yi et al. 2014).

Pulsar emission mechanism

Pulsars are not only excellent tools, they are also sources of multiband electromagnetic radiation. Particularly, pulsars emit relativistic electrons/protons, known as the pulsar wind, which interact with the surrounding materials to give high energy radiation, as in pulsar nabulea and pulsar binaries (Yi & Cheng, 2017).

Gravitational waves

Gravitational waves (GW) are propagating ripples on the space time manifold itself. With the recent detection with LIGO, it became the newly opened channel in astronomy. I'm also very interested in researches on GW related topics. Besides pulsar timing GW detection (Yi et al. 2014), I have also studied on the posibility of GW cherenkov radiation ().
